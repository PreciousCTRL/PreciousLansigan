Childhood

     Precious Leona Guevarra Lansigan, born on August 25, 2004, in Lying in, Marulas, Valenzuela City.1 She grew up in a family of five; her father, Rolando R. Lansigan, her mother, Raquel G. Lansigan, her older brother, Paolo Luis G. Lansigan, and her older sister, Patricia Lorraine G. Lansigan.2 She lived in a neighborhood surrounded by her relatives.3 She was an extroverted child, as her parents often tell, and she wasn’t shy about showing off her talents.4 She was encourage to dance in public or "rumampa”, and she would do it, even if it meant being the center of attention.5 One of her earliest memories is trying to read a book in English, though she didn’t know how to read yet.6 She was babbling the word “heart”, and even though she didn’t understand it, she felt proud to try.7 Apparently, there’s a video of her doing this on the internet, which is both funny and nostalgic to look back on.8 As a child, she had a passion for drawing.9 She spent hours copying drawings she found online, trying to perfect them and proudly showing them to her parents.10 They always encouraged her, and she felt a strong sense of accomplishment when they praised her work.11 This early experience might have sparked her desire for validation, a trait she's still working on today.12 Her childhood wasn’t just about drawing or performing for her family, it was also about friendships.13 She have 2 childhood bestfriends, Adrian, who is a relative of her, and Lara, a friend that lived nearby them.14 They did everything together, from playing outside to having a sleepover at her house.15 Till to this day, their friendship is still strong as ever!16 She studied in St. Mary’s Academy of Caloocan City where she also made friends, some of whom she's still close with today.17 The bond they formed in those early years has endured, and they still keep in touch regularly.18 Her friends and her shared everything, from their favorite games to their personal dreams, and they still connect to this day.19 She had a great time in her childhood, and those were truly some of the most fun and meaningful years of her life.20 Looking back, she's grateful for the relationships she built during that time.21

Teenage

     Her transition to high school was not that tough, at least in terms of changing schools.1 She simply moved from heer old school to the one beside it, Notre Dame of Greater Manila, along with her elementary school friends.2 She actually just followed her sister who was already a student there, and her friends followed her as well.3 It was exciting because it felt like a fresh start in a new environment, even though she was familiar with the place.4 The school was much bigger, with a lot more students, which made it feel like a whole new world to explore.5 She performed academically well in that school, and even made it to the top 40 students in their batch, which really boosted her confidence.6 However, there were some challenges she faced academically that she still deals with today.7 One of the biggest challenges was that she never really learned how to study effectively.8 She mostly relied on answering questions in exams and hoping she got them right, and surprisingly, this strategy worked out well until now.9 She managed to remain a consistent honor student all throughout high school, up until senior high.10 In grade 9, she was chosen, along with others, to join a competition on General Computer Subjects, and they won first place!11 It was an incredible experience, though the pressure of making quick decisions under time constraints was nerve-wracking, which is why she decided not to join any more competitions after that.12 Then, the pandemic hit, and everything changed.13 She was stuck at home for two years, and school became even more challenging.14 Despite the tough situation, Precious surprisingly thrived during that time.15 She was much more active and engaged compared to previous school years.16 In grade 11, Precious was elected vice governor of the class, and in grade 12, she joined the student council.17 Being part of the student council benefited her socially and helped her develop leadership skills.18 It pushed her to interact with students from different year levels, which was something Precious had never done before.19 It also gave her a sense of responsibility and ownership over her school, and she felt like she truly belonged to the community.20 The last achievement Precious earned during high school and senior high school years was receiving the Best Research Award in ICT.21 Winning that award not only validated her hard work but also gave her a sense of accomplishment that she carried into college years with her newfound confidence.22 The pandemic and her involvement in school leadership gave Precious a new perspective on what it meant to be part of something bigger than herself.23

College

     She is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology at the University of the East - Manila.1 It has been a thrilling experience for her, to say the least.2 The university is located far from where she lives, and the culture there is very different from what she is used to.3 Initially, Precious considered other universities like Ateneo and DLSU since she passed their entrance exams, but the scholarships offered by UE were too tempting to ignore.4 Precious believed in herself and knew she could earn those scholarships, which, luckily, she did.5 She wasn’t nervous about starting at UE because her sister also graduated from there, so she had some guidance about how to navigate the campus.6 However, she did feel nervous about interacting with other students.7 Since no one from her old school attends UE, she had to rely on her social skills to make new friends.8 Fortunately, Precious managed to form a group of friends who trust and support each other.9 Academically, she's doing well, not necessarily excelling, but definitely not falling behind either.10 She also joined an organization called Research and Development, where she's part of the research team.11 This organization has been a huge help to her, especially as the workload gets more intense.12 However, she's quite nervous about next school year when they start working on their thesis.13 The pressure is building, and Precious is worried she won’t be able to deliver the way she intend to.14 Despite this, she's determined to push through and overcome her fears by becoming the best version of herself.15 Precious plan to stay focused and maintain a positive state of mind as she tackles the challenges ahead.16 For her future,Precious is not going to lock herself in a single path, rather, she's going to open multiple doors for her to take, for her to explore, so that her future will be as bright as God intended it to be.17 She know that she has been through a lot, but somehow, Precious always made it through.18 Just like before, when she faced challenges and thought she wouldn’t make it, Precious always found a way.19 She remind myself that, no matter what comes next, she have the strength to handle it.20 She know it’ll be okay, like it always has been.21 As she continue this life, she'll take things one step at a time, confident that, in the end, everything will fall into place. 22
